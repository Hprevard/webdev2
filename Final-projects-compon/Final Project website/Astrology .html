Astrology

Astrology is a ancient study and practice on celestial bodies, their movements and their influence on peoples lives. Astrology itself has existed through many cultures for thousands of years. Most people have a lot of skepticism around the validity of astrology and will simply name it a pseudoscience. To contrast most people that genuinely understand astrology point out that it offers great insights into people’s relationship’s, personalities, and their future. The possible evidence for astrology wouldn’t align with the scientific standards most people were used to. Proof of astrology can be contributed to the relevance and historical allure. Astrology has played a huge role in shaping societies and people’s views. In Nicholas Campion’s seminal work “The History of Western Astrology” he traces the origins of Astrology to Mesopotamia and Egyptian civilizations where celestial movement and signs were highly regarded. As astrology was integrated into many different cultures and countries the interest in it grew.

Astrology mainly resonates with people seeking self understanding and meaning in our confusing world. Carl Jung, a psychologist, explained astrology using synchronicities. Where he suggested that people experience coincidences between internal psychological states and external events. In other studies Geoffrey Dean and Ivan W. Kelly explained that astrology is the experience of countless believers to confirm the psychological relevance in giving people comfort, guidance and a sense of belonging. Nowadays astrology has expanded to the modern era while retaining it’s core principles. The internet and social media has given way to more and more audiences to learn and experience astrology. This has caused many different astrology communities of those who love it and practice it. Astrologers like Susan Miller and Chani Nicholas have gained large amounts of recognition for their predictions, forecasts for those seeking guidance. Despite criticism from skeptics, astrology's popularity persists, with surveys indicating significant proportions of the population consulting horoscopes and astrological services. Despite criticism, astrology remains popular. In conclusion astrology does lack normal scientific evidence but it’s historical impact and influence cannot be denied. Throughout everything, astrology still gives meaning and insight to help people navigate through the hardships of life. Whether viewed as relic of history or a tool for personal growth its a phenomenon that reflects our fascination with the universe.
